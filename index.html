Influenza

Cause: (-) RNA virus → Influenza A, B, C, D

Effects: upper respiratory distress

Common names: The Flu

Influenza

  • Antigenic shift and drift are how Influenza viruses are able to evolve so quickly

  • Influenza have segmented genomes that can reassort themselves if two strains infect the same cell

  • Influenza A can do this very rapidly. B can do this quickly. Strains C and D do this very slowly

Influenza

  • Most Flu victims get better within a week, but the disease causes an average of 36,000 deaths per year since 2010.

  • "The Flu" is specified as an A and B strain. Type A mutates very quickly due to cross-species infection.

HIV

Cause: DNA retrovirus via fluid transmission

Effects: Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome

Common names: Hi-Five, AIDS (misnomer), GRID (Outdated c. 1983)

HIV

HIV stands for Human Immunodeficiency Virus, which over time becomes AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome). There is no known cure for HIV/AIDS.

HIV

HIV are retroviruses, which means they integrate thier own genome into the victim's, which is one of many ways a virus can hijack a cell.
